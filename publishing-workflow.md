Publishing Workflow Documentation

Overview

This document outlines the end-to-end workflow involved in processing and publishing scientific and technical manuscripts. It ensures consistency, quality, and timely delivery of content for global publishers.

Workflow Stages

1. Manuscript Submission

   Authors submit manuscripts through submission systems

   Initial checks for completeness and formatting

2. Peer Review Process

	Manuscript assigned to reviewers

	Feedback collected and shared with authors

	Revisions submitted by authors

3. Copyediting

	Language editing for grammar, clarity, and consistency

	Application of style guides (APA, AMA, Chicago, IEEE)

	Formatting as per journal requirements

4. Typesetting

	Conversion into publication format (PDF, XML, HTML)

	Layout adjustments and styling

5. Proofreading

	Final quality check

	Correction of minor errors

	Author approval

6. Publication

	Final article published online or in print

	Metadata indexing and archiving

Tools Used:

Content Management Systems (CMS);
ENOVIA (SPECify);
DigiEdit / PowerEdit;
Microsoft Word / XML tools

Quality Control Measures:

Style guide compliance;
Consistency checks;
KPI-based performance tracking;
Peer review validation

Key Benefits:

Ensures high-quality publication output;
Reduces errors and rework;
Improves turnaround time;
Maintains consistency across documents
